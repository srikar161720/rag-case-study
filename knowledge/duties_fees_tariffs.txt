================================================================================
CUSTOMS BROKERAGE DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE — DUTIES, FEES & TARIFF PROGRAMS
================================================================================

1. HTS CODE (HARMONIZED TARIFF SCHEDULE)
   The classification code that determines the duty rate for imported goods.
   U.S. HTS codes are 10 digits (e.g., 6110.20.2079 — cotton pullovers).
   The first 6 digits follow the international Harmonized System (HS);
   digits 7-8 are U.S.-specific; digits 9-10 are statistical suffixes.

   When reporting, always display HTS codes with dots in the standard format
   (XXXX.XX.XXXX), not as raw 10-digit numbers.

2. PRIMARY DUTY
   The base duty rate applied to goods based on their HTS classification.
   Duty rates vary widely: some goods enter duty-free (0%), while others
   may have rates exceeding 25%. Calculated as: Entered Value × Duty Rate.

3. TARIFF STACKING
   The practice of applying multiple tariff layers to a single entry line.
   A single product may be subject to:
   - Primary duty (based on HTS classification)
   - Section 301 duties (trade remedy against China)
   - Section 232 duties (national security tariffs on steel/aluminum)
   - IEEPA duties (reciprocal tariffs under executive authority)

   Each additional tariff uses a "99 code" (Chapter 99 of the HTS) to
   identify the program. When analyzing total duty spend, all layers must
   be summed.

4. SECTION 301 TARIFFS (CHINA-SPECIFIC)
   Trade remedy tariffs applied to goods of Chinese origin. Key codes:
   - 9903.88.03 (List 3): 25% additional duty on ~$200B of Chinese goods
   - 9903.88.15 (List 4A): 7.5% additional duty on ~$120B of Chinese goods

   RULE: Section 301 tariffs apply ONLY to goods where Country of Origin
   is China (CN). They do not apply to goods manufactured in other countries
   even if components originated in China.

5. IEEPA RECIPROCAL TARIFFS
   Tariffs imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act,
   applied on a country-by-country basis. These are additive to primary
   duty and any Section 301 tariffs. Key codes in this dataset:
   - 9903.01.25: China (20%)
   - 9903.02.46: Vietnam (46%)
   - 9903.02.26: India (26%)
   - 9903.02.32: Indonesia (32%), Taiwan (32%)
   - 9903.02.37: Bangladesh (37%)
   - 9903.02.25: South Korea (25%)

   RULE: IEEPA duties began rolling out in early 2025. Entries before
   February 2025 in this dataset will not have IEEPA duties applied.

6. MPF (MERCHANDISE PROCESSING FEE)
   A CBP-assessed fee on all formal entries (value > $2,500). Calculated at
   0.3464% of the entered value, with:
   - Minimum: $31.67 per entry
   - Maximum: $614.35 per entry

   NOTE: MPF is calculated per ENTRY, not per line. When aggregating at the
   line level, the MPF shown is the line-level allocation. For entry-level
   totals, the capped amount applies.

7. HMF (HARBOR MAINTENANCE FEE)
   A fee assessed on goods arriving by ocean vessel, calculated at 0.125%
   of the entered value. There is no cap. HMF applies only to ocean
   shipments (Mode of Transport = Ocean).

8. ENTERED VALUE
   The declared customs value of imported merchandise, typically the
   transaction value (price actually paid or payable) plus certain
   statutory additions (assists, packing costs, royalties). This is the
   basis for calculating ad valorem duties and fees.

9. TOTAL DUTY, TAXES, FEES & PENALTIES
   The comprehensive total of all financial obligations on an entry:
   Primary Duty + Section 301 Duty + IEEPA Duty + MPF + HMF + any
   additional taxes or penalties. This is the key financial metric for
   customer reporting and QBR (Quarterly Business Review) generation.

================================================================================
BUSINESS RULES FOR ANALYTICS
================================================================================

RULE 1 — Date Filtering
   Always use Release Date for period-based queries unless the user
   explicitly asks for Entry Filed Date or Summary Date.

RULE 2 — Entry Count vs. Line Count
   "How many entries?" = count of DISTINCT entry numbers.
   "How many lines?" or "entry lines?" = count of tariff line records.
   Always clarify which metric is being reported.

RULE 3 — Duty Spend Aggregation
   When asked "total duty spend," sum the "Total Duty, Taxes, Fees &
   Penalties" column. When asked for a duty breakdown, separate into
   Primary Duty, Section 301, IEEPA, MPF, and HMF components.

RULE 4 — Country of Origin Filtering
   Trade remedy tariffs (301, IEEPA) are origin-based. When analyzing
   tariff impact, always group by Country of Origin Code.

RULE 5 — Shell Entries
   Entries with abnormally short entry numbers (e.g., only 3 digits) or
   zero entered values may be "shell entries" — placeholder records created
   when the broker was not the filing party. Exclude these from standard
   analytics unless specifically asked.

RULE 6 — On-Hold Entries
   Entries flagged as "On Hold" are still valid entries but may have
   delayed release or pending government action. Include them in counts
   and financial totals unless the user asks specifically about
   cleared/released entries only.
