CUSTOMER PROFILES & QUARTERLY BUSINESS REVIEW (QBR) METRICS

CUSTOMER: MERIDIAN HOME FURNISHINGS (MHF)
   Industry: Home furnishings and décor
   Primary products: Upholstered seating, wooden furniture, lighting,
   textiles, mirrors, cushions, floor coverings
   Key sourcing countries: China, Vietnam, India, Indonesia
   Notable: High average entered value per entry due to furniture weight
   and bulk. Tariff exposure concentrated in Chapters 94 (furniture) and
   63 (textile furnishings).

CUSTOMER: PACIFIC COAST APPAREL (PCA)
   Industry: Apparel and fashion accessories
   Primary products: Cotton pullovers, t-shirts, trousers, shirts, handbags
   Key sourcing countries: China, Vietnam, Bangladesh, India
   Notable: High entry line counts per shipment due to SKU diversity.
   Significant Section 301 exposure on China-sourced apparel. Duty rates
   in the 16-28% range before trade remedy surcharges.

CUSTOMER: SUMMIT ATHLETIC GEAR (SAG)
   Industry: Sporting goods and athletic wear
   Primary products: Athletic footwear, gloves, sports bags, track suits,
   inflatable balls, caps/hats
   Key sourcing countries: China, Vietnam, Indonesia
   Notable: Footwear duty rates at 20% base, compounded by 301/IEEPA.
   Some product categories (inflatable balls) are duty-free at primary
   level but still subject to trade remedy tariffs.

================================================================================
QBR (QUARTERLY BUSINESS REVIEW) METRICS
================================================================================

The QBR is the primary deliverable for customs analytics clients. It
provides a structured overview of import activity, duty exposure, and
operational efficiency. Standard QBR sections:

1. ENTRY VOLUME SUMMARY
   - Total entries by month and quarter
   - Entry count trend (month-over-month, quarter-over-quarter)
   - Breakdown by port of entry
   - Average lines per entry (indicator of shipment complexity)

2. DUTY SPEND ANALYSIS
   - Total duty, taxes, fees & penalties by period
   - Duty breakdown: Primary vs. Section 301 vs. IEEPA vs. MPF vs. HMF
   - Duty spend by country of origin
   - Top HTS codes by duty contribution
   - Effective duty rate (total duties / total entered value)

3. TARIFF PROGRAM IMPACT
   - Section 301 exposure: total 301 duty, % of total duty spend,
     affected entry count
   - IEEPA reciprocal tariff exposure by country
   - Tariff stacking analysis: entries subject to multiple tariff programs
   - Country-level landed cost comparison

4. OPERATIONAL METRICS
   - Entries on hold: count, hold reasons, average hold duration
   - Entry filing to release time (processing speed)
   - Carrier distribution
   - Port utilization patterns

5. SOURCING INTELLIGENCE
   - Top 10 HTS codes by volume and value
   - Country of origin shift analysis (period-over-period)
   - MID concentration (supplier diversity)
   - Average declared value per unit by product category

================================================================================
METRIC DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

EFFECTIVE DUTY RATE
   Formula: (Total Duties + Fees) / Total Entered Value × 100
   Purpose: Shows the all-in cost of importing as a percentage of goods value.
   A rising effective rate signals increased tariff exposure.

ENTRY LINE DENSITY
   Formula: Total Tariff Lines / Total Distinct Entries
   Purpose: Measures shipment complexity. Higher density means more product
   variety per shipment, which increases classification risk.

SECTION 301 EXPOSURE RATIO
   Formula: Total Section 301 Duty / Total Duty Spend × 100
   Purpose: Shows what portion of a customer's duty bill is attributable
   to the China trade war tariffs. Critical for sourcing shift discussions.

HOLD RATE
   Formula: Entries On Hold / Total Entries × 100
   Purpose: Operational quality indicator. Industry benchmark is < 5%.
   Rates above 8% warrant investigation into compliance issues.
